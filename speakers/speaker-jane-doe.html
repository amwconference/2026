Keynote Talk

"The Future of Graph Databases in AI Foundations"

Abstract: Graph databases are becoming the backbone of modern AI systems. In this keynote, we will explore how we can enable complex reasoning and pattern recognition at an unprecedented scale, transforming the way we understand data management. We will discuss the theoretical foundations of graph neural networks and their integration with traditional relational systems.

Biography

Dr. Jane Doe is a Professor of Computer Science at Stanford University, where she leads the Data Management Research Group. Her research interests lie at the intersection of database systems, knowledge representation, and machine learning.

She has received numerous awards, including the ACM SIGMOD Innovations Award, and has served as the Program Chair for several top-tier conferences. Before joining Stanford, she was a senior researcher at IBM Almaden Research Center.
